Coronavirus spreads globally

Posted on Sat 25 April 2020 in articles

In this post we'll see how coronavirus is spreading around the world over time. The first visualization is a racing bar chart by country. The second visualization is an international geographical mapping. Which visualization do you like better? Comment below!

It has been widely reported that some countries do not have access to the requisite number of tests so they will not appear on the visualizations below. In addition, a recent prevalence study suggests that case counts are vastly under-represented in the Bay Area. Assuming we can trust these reports and studies, case counts are likely significantly more prevalent than the official numbers reported. Therefore, please take these facts into account as we reflect on CoV-2 spread in the visualizations below.

Racing Bar Chart (Flourish Studio)

Creating racing bar charts on Flourish Studio is remarkably easy. After going through the exercise of creating one, I realize now why they have grown so popular on Youtube. The main input is a simple CSV upload and the output, after moving a few columns around, is the attached visualization. Please feel free to explore the visualization to get ideas for creating your own visualizations on Flourish Studio.

World Map (Jupyter Notebooks)

The next video is a geographical mapping of the spread. I built it in Jupyter Notebooks with the Plotly package. If you know Python and are willing to read through some Plotly and/or Matplotlib documentation, much of this visualization is also abstracted for you to construct yourself. Free free to visit my COVID19 Repo to explore the visualization on your own!

Stay safe out there! Next week I hope to do a further deep dive and try to understand the impact of the shelter in place orders across the United States of America.
